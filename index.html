Welcome

The Software Architecture (SWA) research group has been newly founded at the Faculty of Computer Science at RWTH Aachen University in April 2026. The group is headed by Prof. Dr.-Ing. Ronny Seiger.

We investigate topics related to the engineering and development of architectures for modern software systems. A special focus is on software-intensive Cyber-physical Systems (CPS) and Internet of Things (IoT) systems, which combine software and systems engineering aspects. Modern CPS and IoT systems increasingly rely on software to provide innovative features to their users. These cyber-physical software systems need suitable software architectures that go beyond classical systems engineering. Our group applies modern software architecture concepts, techniques, and methods to provide a top-down view on the development of CPS and IoT systems–extending them to the scope of systems of systems. Furthermore, we are investigating how and where artificial intelligence, especially large language models, can be used to efficiently assist software architects with their knowledge-intensive work and decision-making processes.

Research Projects and Topics

  • TaSSAreCt: Towards Sustainable Software Architectures for Cyber-physical Systems of Systems
  • Architectural knowledge management and decision recording
  • Large language models and artificial intelligence in and for software architectures
  • Business process management / workflow systems in modern software architectures
  • Microservices and event-driven architectures
  • Software architectures along the Edge–Cloud continuum
  • Model-driven software architectures and engineering
  • DevOps and containerization
  • Software architectures for CPS and IoT including sensors, actuators, robots, production machines, etc.
  • Domain-specific architectures for, e.g., smart production, smart healthcare, smart homes, smart cities.
